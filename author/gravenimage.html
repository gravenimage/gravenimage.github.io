Elemental words

Published: Tue 25 August 2015
By gravenimage

In misc.

Gearing up to a pub quiz tomorrow night, my friends and I started to have a facebook conversation only using words that could be made from the symbols of the periodic table. (Please don't comment) I spied a chance to ignore my wife for half-an-hour and impress her with my chemical wit afterwards - surely it can't be difficult to create a quick script to generate all the combinations? Spoiler - no, it's easy!

First challenge - getting a list of elements in a convenient format.

http://pastebin.com/raw.php?i=CKwm136x - a quick google and done!

Then, the script. I've cheated here and replaced the csv-parsing with a set constructor - but I did learn about the next method to advance an iteratable (to skip the header on the CSV file). I guess that without more work than I can squeeze in (i.e. not capable of), I'm not going to do better than Python's set union and intersection methods. Ditto for generating the permutations.

import itertools

with open('/usr/share/dict/words') as f:
    words = [s.strip().replace("'", '').lower() for s in f.readlines()]

elements = set(['uuq', 'ge', 'gd', 'ga', 'la', 'li', 'tl', 'tm', 'lr', 'th', 'ti', \
                       'te', 'tb', 'tc', 'ta', 'yb', 'db', 'dy', 'xe', 'h', 'p', 'zn', 'eu', 'zr', \
                       'er', 'ru', 're', 'rf', 'ra', 'rb', 'rn', 'rh', 'be', 'ba', 'uuu', 'uut', \
                       'bh', 'bi', 'uus', 'bk', 'uuo', 'uun', 'uuh', 'br', 'uub', 'c', 'k', 'o', \
                       's', 'w', 'os', 'co', 'cm', 'cl', 'ca', 'pa', 'cf', 'ce', 'cd', 'cs', 'cr', \
                       'cu', 'pr', 'pt', 'pu', 'pb', 'lu', 'pd', 'po', 'pm', 'hs', 'ho', 'hf', 'hg', \
                       'he', 'md', 'mg', 'b', 'f', 'mo', 'mn', 'n', 'mt', 'v', 'ac', 'ag', 'ir', \
                       'am', 'al', 'as', 'ar', 'au', 'at', 'in', 'ni', 'no', 'na', 'nb', 'nd', 'ne', \
                       'uup', 'es', 'np', 'fr', 'sc', 'fe', 'fm', 'i', 'sr', 'kr', 'si', 'u', 'sn', \
                       'sm', 'y', 'sb', 'sg', 'se'])

possibles = set()
for i in range(2, 5):
    combinations = itertools.combinations_with_replacement(elements, i)
    possibles = possibles.union(set([''.join(c) for c in combinations]))

actuals = possibles.intersection(words)

for word in actuals: print word

The set of possibles expands very rapidly, and kills my little Chromebook with n >= 5. So, the first optimization will be to cut down on the number of possibles

  • some words will never contain certain elements. I'm looking at you, Unununium (Uuu) and friends.

  • English words never contain more than N (to be determined) consonants in a row. So, eliminate them too.
